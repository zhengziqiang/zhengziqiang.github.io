Real-time GAN-based Image Enhancement for Robust Underwater Monocular SLAM

Ziqiang Zheng# Zhicho Xin#    Zhibin Yu      Sai-kit Yeung     

Hong Kong University of Science and Technology

Ocean University of China

Abstract


Underwater monocular visual simultaneous localization and mapping (SLAM) plays an important role in underwater-related computer vision and robotic perception. Unlike the autonomous driving setting or aerial environment, it is extremely difficult and challenging to perform robust and accurate underwater monocular SLAM due to the complex underwater environment and the collected critically degraded image quality. The poor visibility, low contrast, and color distortion of the underwater images result in ineffective and insufficient feature matching. To address this issue, we propose to design a real-time generative adversarial network (GAN) based image enhancement to assist in a robust underwater monocular SLAM system. To reduce the network inference time, we conduct knowledge distillation to achieve GAN compression while achieving high fidelity underwater image enhancement. With the introduction of real-time underwater image enhancement, we can greatly promote underwater SLAM performance. Besides, the proposed method is a generic framework, which could be extended to various SLAM systems and achieve performance gain. To the best of our knowledge, we are the first to conduct the real-time GAN-based image enhancement to perform a more robust and accurate underwater SLAM.
